Frequent Questions

What & when is WAGS?

We meet every Wednesday for 10 holes around Sutton Coldfield Golf Course followed by a few beers. .

Does it cost to enter?

Weekly entry fee is £5.00 with £1.50 going to each weeks winner (any draw rolls over to the following weeks kitty) £3.50 goes towards end of season away day and presentation evening.

How does the scoring work?

The game is Stapleford using your WAGS Handicap played over the first 10 holes using WAGS unique Stroke index as below, white tees unless advised different. Hole 1 SI 9 - Hole 2 SI 10 - Hole 3 SI 1 Hole 4 SI 5 - Hole 5 SI 4 Hole 6 SI 2 - Hole 7 SI 7 - Hole 8 SI 3 - Hole 9 SI 6 - Hole 10 SI 8

How are H'CAPS worked out?

New WAGS handicap calculations are 3/4 of your club handicap divided by 2. Category 1 golfers handicap will be increased by 0.1 for any score below par points, outside the buffer zone (which is 19 points) and reduced by 0.1 for each point scored above par. Category 2 golfers handicap will be increased by 0.1 for any score below par points, outside the buffer zone (which is 18 points) and reduced by 0.2 for each point scored above par. Category 3 golfers handicap will be increased by 0.1 for any score below par points, outside the buffer zone (which is 17 points) and reduced by 0.3 for each point scored above par.

What are snakes & camels?

Last player in your group to 3 putt gets the snake and pays an additioanl £1.00 at the end of the round. Last player in your group that ends up in a bunker gets the camel and pays an additioanl £1.00 at the end of the round.

What is Best 14?

There's a prize for the player with the best 14 scores at the end of the season.

What is the RS Cup?

We play an FA Cup style competition throughout the season recently renamed the Richard Sly Cup in memory of a former WAG.

What are the Leagues?

To keep everyones season going we play 6 individual leagues each consisting of 10 players. The top & bottom 3 players swap places at the end of each season.
